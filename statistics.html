Introduction to Statistics

Course Objectives

  • Understand basic statistical concepts and their application in research.
  • Develop skills to summarize and interpret data.
  • Gain knowledge of hypothesis testing and statistical inference.
  • Apply statistical methodologies to real-world research scenarios.

Course Structure

Chapter 1: Measurement Scale of Variables

  • Importance of statistics in research
  • Types of data (quantitative, qualitative)
  • Levels of measurement (nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio)

Activity: Classify and identify data types in sample research scenarios.


Chapter 2: Descriptive Statistics

  • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Measures of dispersion (variance, standard deviation, range)
  • Data visualization (bar charts, histograms, box plots)

Activity: Summarize and visualize a small dataset.


Chapter 3: Probability Concepts

  • Basic probability rules and concepts
  • Probability distributions (normal, binomial, Poisson)
  • Central Limit Theorem

Activity: Solve probability problems related to research experiments.


Chapter 4: Inferential Statistics

  • Concept of population vs. sample
  • Confidence intervals and margins of error
  • Hypothesis testing (null and alternative hypotheses)

Activity: Calculate confidence intervals for sample data.


Chapter 5: Parametric Tests

  • T-tests (one-sample, independent, and paired)
  • Analysis of variance (ANOVA)
  • Assumptions of parametric tests

Activity: Conduct a t-test on a provided dataset.


Chapter 6: Non-Parametric Tests

  • When to use non-parametric tests
  • Chi-square tests for independence and goodness of fit
  • Mann-Whitney U test and Kruskal-Wallis test

Activity: Perform a Chi-square test using research data.


Chapter 7: Correlation and Regression Analysis

  • Correlation analysis
  • Pearson and Spearman correlation coefficients
  • Simple linear regression and interpretation
  • Multiple linear regression and interpretation

Activity: Build and interpret a simple regression model.


Chapter 8: Practical Applications in Research

  • Sampling techniques and sample size calculation
  • Handling missing data and outliers
  • Ethical considerations in statistical analysis

Activity: Critique a research paper's statistical methods.

Interactive Features

  • Hands-on Exercises: Analyze datasets from different domains (e.g., social science, health research).
  • Quizzes: End-of-module quizzes to reinforce learning.
  • Real-World Examples: Discuss statistical methods in published research.
  • Capstone Project: Design a mini-research project with statistical analysis.
