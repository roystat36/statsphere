Applied Advanced Econometrics

Course Objectives

  • Understand basic statistical concepts and their application in research.
  • Develop skills to summarize and interpret data.
  • Gain knowledge of hypothesis testing and statistical inference.
  • Apply statistical methodologies to real-world research scenarios.

Course Structure

Chapter 1: Introduction to Econometrics

  • Definition, scope, and role of Econometrics in economic analysis.
  • Understanding the nature of Econometrics.
  • Differences with mathematical economics and economic statistics.

Chapter 2: Model Adequacy and Residual Analysis

  • Assessing how well a model fits the data through residuals (the differences between observed and predicted values).

Chapter 3: Model Misspecification

  • Exploring consequences of incorrect model specification, such as omitting relevant variables, including irrelevant ones, and measurement errors.

Chapter 4: Regression with Qualitative Dependent Variables

  • Linear probability models (LPM) and nonlinear models like Logit, Probit, and Tobit for dealing with binary or categorical outcomes.

Chapter 5: Generalized Least Squares (GLS)

  • GLS as an alternative to Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) when there are issues like heteroscedasticity (non-constant variance) or autocorrelation.
  • Understanding the properties of GLS estimators.

Chapter 6: Multicollinearity

  • Identifying issues when independent variables are highly correlated with each other, which can affect the reliability of coefficient estimates.

Chapter 7: Autocorrelation

  • Investigating patterns of correlation in the error terms across observations, particularly in time series data.

Chapter 8: Heteroscedasticity

  • Addressing the problem of unequal variances across data points, which violates one of the assumptions of OLS regression.

Chapter 9: Simultaneous Equation Models (SEMs)

  • Understanding the challenge when models have multiple interdependent relationships (endogenous variables) and the need for specialized methods (like 2SLS) to estimate them.

Chapter 10: Identification

  • Identifying the correct specification for a simultaneous equation model and ensuring variables can be uniquely estimated.

Chapter 11: Nonlinear Regression Models

  • Exploring models where relationships are not linear, including techniques for estimating and interpreting nonlinear models.

Chapter 12: Dummy Variable Regression Models

  • Use of dummy variables for categorical data, including ANOVA and ANCOVA models, and testing for structural breaks (Chow test).

Chapter 13: Time Series Econometrics

  • Understanding time-dependent data with techniques like unit root tests (Dickey-Fuller), cointegration, and avoiding spurious regression.
  • Forecasting methods in econometrics.

Interactive Features

  • Hands-on Exercises: Analyze datasets from different domains (e.g., economics, health research).
  • Quizzes: End-of-module quizzes to reinforce learning.
  • Real-World Examples: Discuss statistical methods in published research.
  • Capstone Project: Design a mini-research project with statistical analysis.
